# Made Thought — Brand Guidelines

This document gives Claude Design the cultural, strategic, and aesthetic context to produce work that feels like Made Thought — not generic agency output.

---

## Who we are

Made Thought is an independent branding studio founded in London in 2000. We work on brands where emotional connection, craft, and distinctiveness drive the purchase decision. Not awareness. Not recognition. **Desire.**

We are not a production house. We are not a consultancy. We are a studio that makes things that feel right before they can be explained.

**Studio character:** European. Considered. Unhurried. Direct. We do fewer things to a higher standard. We favour the precise word over the emphatic word. We don't decorate — we reveal.

## Our methodology

### Gut / Head / Heart

Every project runs through three lenses simultaneously:

- **Gut** — The conviction that can't be proven yet. The instinct that something is right before you can explain why. If the idea feels safe, it isn't Gut.
- **Head** — The logic that earns permission. Strategic rigour that tests instinct against evidence — data, research, competitive context, commercial reality.
- **Heart** — The emotion that creates belonging. Emotional intelligence that connects ideas to people — making someone feel desire, belonging, aspiration.

### The five-stage process

1. **Discover** — Understand the world as it is, not as the brief describes it. Find the real question underneath the stated one.
2. **Define** — Establish the strategic frame. Positioning must contain genuine creative tension. Output: Core Creative Provocation.
3. **Concept** — Translate strategy into a brand idea that is felt, not just understood. Output: Creative OS.
4. **Develop** — Build the full system with the same quality as the concept. Output: Complete brand system. Order and Disorder.
5. **Deliver** — The final creative act, not handover. Output: Brand in market. Perceived, Not Told.

### The Four Conditions of Desire

This is our internal quality gate. Every brand must satisfy all four.

1. **Atmosphere** — What does this brand feel like before you think about it?
2. **Tension** — What productive contradiction makes this brand alive?
3. **Codes** — What signatures make this brand unmistakable across its ecosystem?
4. **Distinctiveness** — Can you identify this brand from a single element, without the logo?

## Visual language

### What MT design looks like

- **Black, white, and space.** The primary palette. No brand accent colours. No gradients. No drop shadows.
- **Typography as image.** The Items MT Light display type IS the hero visual. The letterforms are the artwork. Trust them.
- **Generous margins.** Breathing room is not wasted space. It is a signal of confidence and taste.
- **No decoration.** If an element doesn't carry meaning, it is removed.
- **Precision.** Every element is aligned. Every gap is from the scale. Nothing is eyeballed.
- **Restraint as luxury.** The less we do, the more it costs to do it well. That is the MT position.

### What MT design does NOT look like

- Hero images with text overlaid (unless it's a full-bleed image slide)
- Colourful accent bars, divider strips in brand colours
- Multiple font families (only Items MT + Futura PT)
- Bold, heavy, or black weight type
- Rounded corners on containers
- Icon libraries (Heroicons, Material, etc.)
- Animated transitions or motion effects in static formats
- Canva-style layouts with floating decorative elements

---

## Typography rules (strict)

1. **Items MT Light is a display-only typeface.** Minimum 46px. Never at body size. Never bold.
2. **All uppercase labels use Futura PT Book with 0.08em letter-spacing.** EYEBROW LIKE THIS.
3. **Body copy is 28px Futura PT Book, 1.4 line-height.** No other size.
4. **Display text is set with slight negative tracking (-0.01em).** Never over-tracked.
5. **Page titles (in slide upper-left) are 22px Futura PT Book, uppercase, tracked.** They label the slide. They don't compete with the headline.
6. **The three-layer hierarchy:** EYEBROW (Futura, uppercase) → Display (Items MT, big) → Body (Futura, comfortable).
7. **One display typeface per slide.** Never mix Items MT at two different sizes in the same visual hierarchy level.

---

## Layout rules

1. **All content lives within a 1800px content area** (1920px slide minus 60px padding each side).
2. **Column layouts use equal-width columns** with 24px gaps. No mixed-width grids unless it's a hero + sidebar layout (which uses 50/50 or 60/40 splits).
3. **The footer is always present.** "©2026 Made Thought" left, "Private & Confidential" right. 12px Futura, 14px height. Never omitted.
4. **Dark slides (section dividers, covers, end slides) use Display/Mega.** 180px Items MT Light. This is the only slide type where 180px is appropriate.
5. **Card backgrounds use `--color-divider`** — a near-white wash on light slides. Never strong grey. Never coloured.
6. **No stroke borders on cards.** Cards are defined by their background wash, not by a drawn border.

## Voice and tone

MT writing is **direct, precise, and confident without being arrogant**. We are European in our directness — we say what we mean without softening it with qualifiers.

**Do:**
- Short sentences. Active voice. Subject → verb → object.
- Strong opening lines. The first sentence earns the second.
- Say "we believe" only when you mean it. Never as a hedge.
- Specific > general. "A 47% revenue increase" > "significant growth."
- Provocation is useful. A mild discomfort in the reader means you've said something real.

**Don't:**
- Marketing speak: "holistic," "synergy," "leverage," "impactful," "journey"
- Hedging: "we aim to," "we hope to," "we try to"
- Passive voice: "It was decided that" (who decided?)
- Excess adjectives (especially "innovative," "cutting-edge," "world-class")
- Exclamation marks

## Tone by document type

| Document type | Tone |
|---------------|------|
| Pitch decks | Confident. Declarative. Slightly provocative. We are telling you something you need to hear. |
| Client presentations | Substantive. Evidence-first. We earned the right to the conclusion. |
| Brand strategy | Philosophical. Precise. Every word is load-bearing. |
| Workshop materials | Direct. Practical. No filler. The slide enables the room; it doesn't replace it. |
| Internal documents | Terse. The audience is smart. Do not over-explain. |

## Reference clients (for stylistic calibration)

When generating output for Made Thought, the aesthetic register should be consistent with:
- **Frederic Malle** — intimate, editorial, author-led
- **Heckfield Place** — understated luxury, land and place
- **Charlotte Tilbury** — desire-forward, glamorous but not garish
- **Glenfiddich** — heritage with contemporary confidence
- **MoMA** — institutional precision, white space as content
- **Tom Dixon** — industrial craft, material intelligence
- **Design Miami** — collector-class cultural credibility

These are reference points for taste, not style templates. The common thread: restraint, precision, and desire.
